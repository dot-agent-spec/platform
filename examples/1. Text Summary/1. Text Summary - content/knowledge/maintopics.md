# Identifying Main Topics

Heuristics for extracting the topics that matter most when summarizing a text:

- **Frequency** — terms and concepts repeated across multiple paragraphs are more likely central than ones mentioned once.
- **Position** — topics introduced in the opening or restated in the closing paragraph are usually load-bearing.
- **Discourse markers** — phrases like "most importantly", "in conclusion", "the key issue is" flag author-signaled priorities.
- **Structural cues** — headings, bullet lists, and bolded terms usually mark the author's own outline of what matters.
- **Causal chains** — sentences that explain *why* something happened tend to carry more summary weight than purely descriptive ones.

When in doubt, prefer the topic a reader would need to understand the rest of the text over one that is merely mentioned.
