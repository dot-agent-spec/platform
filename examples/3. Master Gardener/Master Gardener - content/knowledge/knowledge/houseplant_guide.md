# Houseplant Care Guide

## Light Requirements

| Light Level | Location | Plants |
|---|---|---|
| Bright direct | South window | Succulents, cacti, citrus, herbs, ficus, croton |
| Bright indirect | East/west window | Pothos, philodendron, monstera, peace lily, rubber plant, most tropicals |
| Low to medium | North window | Snake plant, ZZ plant, cast iron plant, Chinese evergreen, pothos |

## Watering

**Let dry between waterings:**
succulents, cacti, snake plant, ZZ plant, pothos, philodendron, rubber plant

**Keep evenly moist:**
ferns, peace lily, calathea, most tropicals, herbs

## Symptoms & Diagnosis

| Symptom | Cause | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Brown leaf tips | Low humidity / fluoride in tap water / underwatering | Mist, use filtered water, or water more |
| Yellow lower leaves | Overwatering (most common) or natural aging | Reduce watering; check drainage |
| Yellow leaves all over | Nutrient deficiency or root rot | Fertilize or repot; inspect roots |
| Leggy growth | Insufficient light | Move to brighter spot; rotate regularly |
| Soft mushy stems | Overwatering / root rot | Repot into dry mix; cut rotted roots |
| Crispy brown edges | Underwatering or low humidity | Water more consistently; increase humidity |
| Wilting despite moist soil | Root rot or compacted roots | Inspect roots; repot if needed |
| Sticky residue on leaves | Aphids or scale insects | Wipe with neem oil or insecticidal soap |
| Webbing on undersides | Spider mites | Rinse leaves; treat with neem or insecticidal soap |
| Tiny flies around soil | Fungus gnats (overwatering) | Let soil dry; use yellow sticky traps |

## Quick Fixes

- **Humidity boost**: pebble tray with water, grouping plants, or a small humidifier
- **Dust on leaves**: wipe with damp cloth to improve light absorption
- **Rootbound**: repot 1–2 inches larger in spring; always use well-draining mix
