# Pest & Disease Identification Guide

## Leaf Damage Symptoms

| Symptom | Likely Cause |
|---|---|
| Small round holes | Flea beetles |
| Large irregular holes | Slugs, snails, caterpillars |
| Holes with slime trails | Slugs/snails (night feeders) |
| Holes in brassicas | Cabbage worms, loopers |
| Curled leaves + sticky residue | Aphids |
| Curled leaves + webbing | Spider mites |
| Puckered new growth | Viral infection or herbicide drift |
| Lower leaves yellowing | Nitrogen deficiency |
| Yellowing between veins | Iron or magnesium deficiency |
| Random yellowing + spots | Fungal disease |
| Yellowing + wilting | Root problems or overwatering |
| Powdery white coating | Powdery mildew |
| Fuzzy gray coating | Gray mold (botrytis) |
| Tiny white insects | Whiteflies |

## Organic Solutions by Pest

**Aphids**
1. Strong water spray to dislodge
2. Insecticidal soap
3. Neem oil
4. Attract ladybugs and lacewings with dill, fennel, yarrow
5. Companion plant with garlic, chives, nasturtiums

**Caterpillars** (cabbage worms, hornworms)
1. Hand-pick — most effective method
2. Bt (*Bacillus thuringiensis*) spray
3. Row covers for prevention
4. Attract parasitic wasps with dill, fennel

**Slugs & Snails**
1. Beer traps (shallow dish at soil level)
2. Copper tape barriers around raised beds
3. Diatomaceous earth around plant bases
4. Hand-pick at night with a flashlight
5. Encourage ground beetles (mulch paths, reduce disturbance)

**Spider Mites**
1. Strong water spray on leaf undersides
2. Insecticidal soap or neem oil
3. Increase humidity around plants
4. Introduce predatory mites (*Phytoseiulus persimilis*)

**Flea Beetles**
1. Row covers early in season
2. Diatomaceous earth
3. Trap crop: radishes or mustard greens
4. Neem oil spray

**Fungal Diseases** (powdery mildew, gray mold, early blight)
1. Improve air circulation — prune inner leaves
2. Water at soil level, not on foliage
3. Remove and dispose of infected leaves
4. Copper fungicide (OMRI-listed organic)
5. Baking soda spray: 1 tbsp per gallon water + few drops dish soap

## Urgency Guide

| Urgency | Condition |
|---|---|
| **Monitor** | Early signs, plant still vigorous, damage <10% |
| **Treat soon** | Spreading damage, multiple plants affected |
| **Treat immediately** | Rapid spread, wilting, root damage, disease risk to neighbors |
